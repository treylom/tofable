# Water Propagation Basics

The simplest way to start most soft-stemmed plants (pothos, philodendron,
tradescantia): cut just below a node, strip the lowest leaf, and set the
node in a jar of water. Change the water every few days. Most plants show
root nubs within 1-2 weeks and are ready to pot into soil once roots are
1-2 inches long.

Works well for: pothos, philodendron, tradescantia, coleus.
Doesn't work well for: thick woody stems, most succulents (they tend to
rot in standing water).

See also: [[Soil Propagation Basics]] for plants that root faster direct
in soil, and [[Leaf-Cutting Propagation]] for succulents.
