# Leaf-Cutting Propagation

For succulents and a handful of tropicals (like certain begonias), a
single healthy leaf can grow its own roots and eventually a new small
plant, without needing a stem at all. Twist a leaf off cleanly at its
base, let the wound callus over for 1-2 days, then lay it on top of
well-draining soil (don't bury it). Mist lightly every few days. A tiny
new rosette forms at the base of the leaf over several weeks; the original
leaf shrivels once the new plant is established and can be removed.

Works well for: echeveria, sedum, some begonias.
Doesn't work well for: most vining plants, anything without fleshy
leaves.

See also: [[Water Propagation Basics]] and [[Soil Propagation Basics]] for
stem-based methods.
