# Soil Propagation Basics

Some plants root faster directly in soil than in water, and skip the
"leggy water roots that struggle to adapt to soil" problem entirely. Take
a cutting below a node, dip in rooting hormone (optional but helps), and
plant directly into a small pot of well-draining mix. Keep the soil evenly
moist (not soggy) and cover with a clear bag or dome for the first 1-2
weeks to hold humidity.

Works well for: pilea, most herbs, snake plant sections.

See also: [[Water Propagation Basics]] for soft-stemmed plants that do
better rooting in water first, and [[Leaf-Cutting Propagation]] for
succulents.
